# Mael Schuler

### About Me
I am a fourth-year student at Trinity University graduating in May of 2027. My academic focus is on mathematics, finance, economics, and data analysis/visualization. My inspiration to work in this industry comes from a deep curiosity about how we use statistics and data to make important investment, business, and legislative decisions. 

I have completed coursework in classes such as _Studies of Urban Inequalities_, using Stata to create simple regressions and graphs that visualize differences in urban/suburban/rural populations in the United States (controlling for variables such as gender, race, parental income, educational attainment, etc.). Afterward, I was responsible for creating a write-up on my project and leading a lecture-discussion on the topic, which taught me the importance of digesting and articulating large datasets and conclusions (and comparing them to empirical evidence).

Looking back on that class, my past methodology now feels quite rudimentary. I have since expanded my understanding of applied probability and statistics to a much greater dimension, thanks to recent classes such as _Econometrics_ and _Probability_. The culmination of my _Econometrics_ class was my creation of a synthetic control model to visually examine the efficacy of recent Colorado legislation on the private health insurance market.
